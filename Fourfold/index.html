Fourfold is a collaborative video installation by Zhengyang Huang and Zhengzhou Huang. It is an study of imagery surface and its continuation of folding onto physical materials.


Fourfold's narrative is based on a Chinese board game, mahjong, in which the four players, by drawing mahjong tiles, manage to form a complete set of patterns. This work is set up in four domestic rooms. In each room, a set of wooden stools, stands and fabric screens are placed to form a furniture object. On each furniture object there is a screen where a video is projected onto. Each video shows one of the four players in two filmic settings.


One setting is brightly green, where the hard, green surface is touched by the players. The other is absorbingly red, where the soft, red drapery covers the filmic space. The flatness, the smooth transition, and the saturated color create a sense of materiality in filmic language, which is similar to the physical materiality of fabric. The projected filmic materiality is continued onto the surface of the furniture object--the orange-stained wood and the red/green fabric screens. Throughout the four rooms, a continuum of surfaces is formed.
