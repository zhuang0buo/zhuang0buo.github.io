Intersurfaces is a group of 2 projects that interact with imagery materials through physical interfaces.



1. A video plays on a screen installed on top of a blue cart.

The video is shot inside a driving car with a blue, paper-folded lotus flower in the middle of the image. The blue lotus is placed in front of the windshield. As the car moves forward in the video, the lotus implies a surface as it bobs and glides in front of the moving roads. The moving scene seen through the driving car acts like a flat, flowing surface on top of which the lotus flower floats.

The cart is padded with thick, protective cushions and people are invited to kick it. The direction of the physical and the virtual movement are similar yet perpendicular to each other. The bobbing and gliding of the lotus interplays with the cart bumping into a physical wall: the virtual movement is activated by our bodies' actions.



2. An interactive weather report featuring a stuffed piglet toy for an empty chair: a monitor on the back of the chair and a speaker in the seat.

Sitting opposite to the chair, you can move closer to the two sides of the chair and blow air closely at its two “ears”---two small, round vibration sensors. Staying close to a chair and blows air into it becomes an intimate gesture with a missing body (virtual body). The air from one’s mouth becomes weather in the video, like the rain the piglet summons or the wind that blows it away.

This rather body-to-body interaction is carried over among a body, materials, and an imagery piggy plush. Though, if two people blow at the two sensors on either side of the chair, the piglet will hide itself, making the system pause for five minutes.
