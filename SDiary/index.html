SDiary is the second diary for Second Life (an online virtual world, developed and owned by the San Francisco-based firm Linden).



This undone project focuses on digital bodies and identities in the atmosphere of personal agency, systematic control, aesthetic ideals and 3D rebdered reality with posts from Second Life forums, as I collect player's posts and rewrite / reimagine them into diaries of avatars: after compromises between personal agency and systematic regulation, avatars comes further to life as textual narratives.



SDiary is embeded in the 3D interface mimicing an operating system. The interfaces shifts between a 2D backdrop resembling the famous Windows desktop image named "Bliss" and a 3D grassy land where you read the diaries. It is a hopeful and pristine land we found ourselves in at the start of Web 2.0. From here, plugins and meshes are incubated, along with aesthetics and blogs forming the atmosphere: it is both a personal story and a communal (should this software be an online activity) environment.
