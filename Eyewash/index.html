Eyewash is a video projected onto a sink-full of still water dyed with black ink---a vitrual reflection and peephole.


The video in Eyewash is shot through a peephole lens which gives the video a circular shape. While being projected onto the water surface, the video becomes a digital peephole for the viewer in the water.

The black-dyed water is so still and reflective that it almost becomes a solid, while the light of the video layers a soft, moving surface on the dark solid surface of water. In the video the peephole lens is constantly being touched, blocked, wiped, blurred, stained, and cleaned, as if the membrane of water is touched from beneath.


Eyewash is also a virtual mirror / reflection It asks questions regarding your personal information, like those questions you would fill out when applying for a driver's license. It reaches out for you at the other end of the peephole.
