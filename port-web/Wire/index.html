Wire is a series of three masks made with wires and mirrors that plays with perspective and vision.



The first mask is for one person, the next for two people sitting side by side, and the third for two people sitting opposite each other. On the masks, fragments of mirrors and glass are positioned close to one’s face making the reflection of the face appears as though at the edge of one’s eyes. The fragmented sections of the face on the mirrors integrate with fragments of what is perceived through the glass to form a textural layer. In this way, the masks blur its wearer’s frontal eye field yet makes their peripheral vision more obvious or even obtrusive. It is through this textural layer the wearer looks out and sees their own faces at the same time.
